Introduction to Communication and Ethics

Syllabus

Tense and types of tenses, The Sequence of Tenses (Rules in use of Tenses) and Exercises on it. Introduction to Phonetics: Introduction, Phonetic Transcription, English Pronunciation, Pronunciation Guidelines Related to consonants and vowels, Sounds Mispronounced, Silent and Non-silent Letters, Syllables and Structure, Word Accent and Stress Shift, Rules for Word Accent, Intonation, Exercises on it. Common Errors in Pronunciation., Useful Practices of Grammar Articles and Prepositions, Subject-verb agreement, noun-pronoun agreement, Oral Communication Listening skills, Public speaking.

Teaching Methodology

The sessions will be conducted in both lecture-style teaching and interactive, activity-based learning. The emphasis will be more on activities.

Faculty

  • prof. Ms. Preeti Shukla

Learning Outcome

  • Understand key theories and models of communication.
  • Analyze the ethical dimensions of communication in personal, professional, and societal contexts.
  • Develop critical thinking and ethical reasoning skills.
  • Public speaking skills.
  • Communicate effectively and ethically in diverse environments.
